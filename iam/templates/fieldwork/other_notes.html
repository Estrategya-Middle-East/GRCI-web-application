  • Input Historical audit findings, control documentation, regulatory requirements.
  • Processing Assess risks and control effectiveness, prioritize entities for audits.

Instructions

  1. List all potential risks for each auditable entity based on historical findings and risk categories.
  2. Review existing control documentation to understand mitigation measures.
  3. Use a risk matrix to evaluate inherent risk (likelihood x impact).
  4. Assess the effectiveness of each identified control using test results.
  5. Calculate residual risk by subtracting control effectiveness from inherent risk.
  6. Assign risk scores (High, Medium, Low) based on the residual risk.
  7. Prepare a summary report highlighting critical risks requiring immediate action.
  8. Share the risk assessment with the audit team for review and input.
  9. Use software tools to document and track risk assessment results.
  10. Record findings in the Risk Assessment Form.
